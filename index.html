About


Hi there, I'm Alex!


My name is Alexander Lipianu and I am a Software Developer from Ontario, Canada.

I am a first-year student pursuing a Bachelor of Computer Science at the University of Waterloo. Passionate about software, my learning doesn’t stop in the classroom. In my spare time I love to experiment with different languages, frameworks, and areas of software development – always eager to expand my horizons and push the boundaries.

Currently, I am working at CareGo Tek Inc. as an Application Developer Co-op.

Previously, I have interned at InFlight Corporation and done simulation work for the WATonomous student design team.

Experience

As a software developer, I believe it is important to be flexible, adaptable, and knowledgeable of a wide range of languages, tools, frameworks, and systems. That is why I have taken it upon myself to gain as much experience as possible, both inside and outside the workplace, in several different areas of software development. Toggle the icons below to learn about my experiences in each area, as well as my proficiency in some of the languages and tools I have worked with.


Testing

Having spent two summers at InFlight Corporation developing internal testing tools for enterprise systems, testing serves as my backbone and one of my earliest exposures to software development. Using C# and .NET, I fully redesigned the UI of and added Google Chrome support to our internal demoing tool, as well as added several querying capabilities to InFlight’s Fiddler debugging extension. At InFlight, I also developed a black-box testing framework for large-scale automated system testing using JavaScript, Node.js, and Mocha, and conducted functional testing of HTTP requests, SSO, JSON objects, and webpage elements.


Web

In my spare time I have strived to gain web development experience by building and maintaining this website using HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript and jQuery. I look forward to working with backend development in the future.


Mobile

Recently, I began experimenting with cross-platform mobile development using my pre-existing windows development skills. I am currently working on some mobile projects with C# and Xamarin, and plan to experiment with native Android and IOS development in the future.

Graphics
& Games

Graphics and games have always been two of my greatest passions, as I’ve always been fascinated with low-level, cutting-edge computation. In high school, my interest in low-level graphics programming drew me to DirectX 12 - reading and working through Frank D. Luna’s 3D Programming with DirectX 12. During my 1A term, I worked on a student design team called WATonomous at the University of Waterloo, where I focused on vehicle camera simulation using C++ and Unreal Engine. Having recently finished Imgrypt, a steganography program that hides messages in image pixel data data, I have moved on to developing a sequel to my first videogame, Paint Street Fighter, using C++ and SFML (a library based on OpenGL).
