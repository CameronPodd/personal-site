About


Hi there, I'm Alex!


My name is Alexander Lipianu and I am a Software Developer from Ontario, Canada.

I am a first-year student pursuing a Bachelor of Computer Science at the University of Waterloo. Passionate about software, my learning doesn’t stop in the classroom. In my spare time I love to experiment with different languages, frameworks, and areas of software development – always eager to expand my horizons and push the boundaries.

Previously, I have interned for InFlight Corporation and done simulation work for the WATonomous student design team.

Starting April, I will be working at CareGo Tek as an Application Developer intern.

Experience

As a software developer, I believe it is important to be flexible, adaptable, and knowledgeable of a wide range of languages, tools, frameworks, and systems. That is why I have taken it upon myself to gain as much experience as possible, both inside and outside the workplace, in several different areas of software development. Toggle the icons below to learn about my experiences in each area, as well as my proficiency in some of the languages and tools I have worked with.


Testing

 Having spent two summers at InFlight Corporation developing and extending internal software testing tools to help improve millions of PeopleSoft user experiences across the world, testing serves as my backbone and one of my earliest exposures to software development. With C# and .NET, I fully redesigned the UI of and added Google Chrome support to our internal demoing tool, as well as added several querying capabilities to InFlight’s Fiddler debugging extension. At InFlight, I also solely developed an automated black-box testing framework using JavaScript, Node.js, and Mocha, and wrote hundreds of black-box tests now ran by the company’s build system before each deployment.


Web

 In my spare time I have worked towards gaining web development experience by building and maintaining this website using HTML, CSS, Bootstrap and JavaScript. Although all of my web development experience currently involves front-end work, I look forward to learning backend development in the future.


Mobile

 Recently, I began experimenting with cross-platform mobile development using my pre-existing windows development skills. Currently, I am working on some mobile projects with C# and Xamarin, and plan to experiment with native Android and IOS development soon.

Graphics
& Games

 Graphics and games have always been two of my greatest passions, as I’ve always been very fascinated with low-level, cutting-edge computation. In high school, my interest in low-level graphics drew me to DirectX12 as I set out to understand how they work, reading and working through Frank D. Luna’s 3D Programming with DirectX 12. During my 1A term, I worked on a student design team called WATonomous at the University of Waterloo, where I focused on vehicle camera simulation using C++ and Unreal Engine. Having recently finished Imgrypt, a steganography program that hides messages in the pixel data of images, I have moved on to developing a sequel to my first videogame, Paint Street Fighter, using C++ and SFML (a library based on OpenGL).

Imgrypt

 Steganography program that conceals and reveals messages in the pixel data of images, leaving no noticeable pixel noise. Encrypted pixel data is uncrackable and passwords have up to a 1 in 1027 chance of being cracked.

Tools: C#/.NET

Platform: PC
